The Golden Ratio Lock-in:
A Mathematical Proof of φ's Inevitability in Recognition Science

Abstract: We present a rigorous proof that the golden ratio φ = (1+√5)/2 is mathematically inevitable in any self-similar recognition system. Using only the axiom of self-similarity and the additivity of recognition costs, we demonstrate that φ is the unique scaling factor that emerges from the fundamental structure of pattern recognition.

The complete paper demonstrates that any self-similar recognition system with additive costs must scale by exactly φ.

Main Theorem (Golden Ratio Lock-in): The unique scaling factor λ in the self-similarity axiom equals the golden ratio φ.

Key insight: By applying self-similarity twice and using cost additivity, we derive that λ² = λ + 1, which has the unique solution λ = φ for λ > 1.

This transforms φ from an empirical observation to a logical necessity in physics.
